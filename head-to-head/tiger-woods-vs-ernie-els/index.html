Key Head-to-Head Moments

2000 U.S. Open: Woods wins at Pebble Beach by a record 15 strokes. Els finishes second but is effectively in a different tournament, encapsulating Tiger's dominance.
2002 Open Championship: Els wins the Claret Jug at Muirfield in a playoff, one of the few times he prevailed with Woods in the field at a major.
2003 Presidents Cup: Woods and Els trade blows in a dramatic singles match that ends all square, symbolic of their mutual respect and competitive fire.
2004 WGC-American Express: Els edges Woods by one shot at Mount Juliet, proving he could go toe-to-toe with Tiger at his peak.
The What-If Factor: Els won four majors but many believe his total would have been higher without Woods. He finished second six times at majors, often with Tiger ahead.
