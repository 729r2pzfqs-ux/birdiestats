September 21 in Golf History

On September 21 in golf history: 5 notable events, spanning 1934–2008.

On This Day in Golf

1934 Milestone

Don Bies; tour and senior winner

American golfer Don Bies won on the PGA Tour and became a multiple winner on the Senior Tour during a long professional career.

1980 Milestone

Henrik Stenson era roots; future Open champ

Sweden's Henrik Stenson, who won the 2016 Open Championship at Royal Troon with a record 264, became one of Europe's finest ball-strikers.

Henrik Stenson
1980 Milestone

Felipe Aguilar; Chilean golf

Chilean golfer Felipe Aguilar won multiple European Tour titles, helping carry the banner for South American golf in the 21st century.

2003 Milestone

Shaun Micheel wins PGA Championship era

Shaun Micheel sealed his stunning 2003 PGA Championship at Oak Hill with a 7-iron to inches on the 72nd hole, his only PGA Tour win.

Shaun Micheel
2008 Championship

USA wins Ryder Cup at Valhalla

Boo Weekley, Anthony Kim and an inspired American side captained by Paul Azinger clinched the 2008 Ryder Cup at Valhalla, the United States' first win since 1999.

Paul Azinger
