July 30 in Golf History

On July 30 in golf history: 6 notable events, including 1 golfer birthday, spanning 1980–2010.

On This Day in Golf

1980 Born

Justin Rose born in Johannesburg

Justin Rose, the 2013 U.S. Open champion and 2016 Olympic gold medalist who reached World No. 1, was born on this day in South Africa before representing England.

Justin Rose
1989 Milestone

Mark Calcavecchia's playoff Open

Mark Calcavecchia's 1989 Open win at Royal Troon came in the championship's first four-hole aggregate playoff, beating Greg Norman and Wayne Grady.

Mark Calcavecchia
1995 Milestone

John Daly's improbable Open

John Daly's 1995 Open win at St Andrews, his second major after the 1991 PGA, came via a playoff over Costantino Rocca on golf's most famous links.

John Daly
2000 Milestone

Tiger Woods's record St Andrews romp

Tiger Woods's 2000 Open win at St Andrews, by eight at 19-under, made him the youngest career Grand Slam winner and confirmed his historic dominance.

Tiger Woods
2006 Milestone

Tiger Woods's emotional Hoylake win

Tiger Woods's 2006 Open win at Royal Liverpool, achieved by strategically avoiding driver on the firm course, came shortly after the death of his father Earl.

Tiger Woods
2010 Milestone

Louis Oosthuizen's St Andrews breakthrough

Louis Oosthuizen's seven-shot 2010 Open win at St Andrews was a surprise runaway that announced him as a major force in world golf.

Louis Oosthuizen
